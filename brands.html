1. Levi’s – Quality never goes out of style

Levi Strauss & Co. or Levi’s is one of the American clothing companies that are famous across the world for its variety and trademark fabric. It is primarily known for its denim which are available for all the ages and genders. Apart from denim, the brand also has an array of shirts, t-shirts, underwear, shoes, accessories, skirts, overalls, jumpsuits, etc. And, not to forget it has a variety of jeans.

3. Van Heusen- – Evolve Yourself

The brand is owned by PVH and is backed by the Madura Fashion and Lifestyle. Undoubtedly, it is one of the top clothing brands in India. The brand is popular for its formal clothing. It has a classic collection of business attire.

4. Park Avenue – Play the Lead

Park Avenue is a one-stop destination for all the men out there who demands sharp looks. It is one of the premium lifestyle brands in the country. The designs of this brand reflect international fabric, color, and fashion trends. Owing to its commendable work in the fashion industry, the brand has won an award for ‘Best Design Concept’ in the year 2015. The brand is owned by the Raymond Group. Besides apparel for men, the brand has also had fragrance, skin, and hair products for men.

5. Mufti – Alternative Clothing

Introduced in the year 1998, with the aim to provide an alternative dress solution, today, Mufti has become one of the top-rated clothing brands in India. The brand is completely dedicated to men’s fashion. It also provides a wide range of dress materials. The list of fashion wear available on Mufti has outerwear, joggers, t-shirts, shirts, blazers, and accessories as well.

6. Biba

Known for its traditional outfits for women, Biba is one of the best clothing brands in India for ethnic wear. The brand was started in the year 1988 in New Delhi. Biba has over 150 brand outlets, along with the 225 multi-brand outlets.

7. W

We are the only brand in the Indian retail space which offers ‘Indian contemporary’ wear and stays a step ahead of the fashion curve. The pioneer in introducing the concept of ‘Mix n Match’ in retail, W designers take inspiration from the latest fashion trends & forecasts from the west and transform them into silhouettes and styles acceptable to the modern Indian woman
